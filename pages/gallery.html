  • The Galápagos are an archipelago of volcanic islands located in the Pacific Ocean right in the Equatoor Line, 605 miles to the west of South America.

  • View of two beaches on Bartolome Island in the Galápagos Islands in Ecuador

  • The Galápagos Islands are famous for exotic birds, tortoises, and iguanas, but recently the archipelago had become overrun with more prosaic animals: rats and mice. Rodents that came on old sailing ships.

  • The islands straddle the equator and are located on both Southern Hemisphere and Northern Hemisphere, with the volcanoes Ecuador and Mount Whiton (highest peak on the Galapagos) located on the equator
